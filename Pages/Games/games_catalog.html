Autastic Education - ABA Therapy App

The Autastic project consists of two primary applications, as well as this application there is a companion application (found under programming). This is the ABA therapy supplement app meant to be used by autistic children. I have been the director and technical lead on the project, coordinating efforts across the team. As well as my managerial role in this project, I have worked on individual levels and technologies used across the project.

This application gamifies and digitizes a selection of ABA therapy activities such that a child and their family can utilize these therapies at home each day, and at a dynamic pace appropriate to that child.

The Autastic project is an ongoing project. As such the images here are from a development build and do not necessarily reflect the finished product.

Wilderness Survival - sandbox survival simulator

Wilderness Survival is an open world survival game that I built for the national FBLA competition my senior year of High School. The game features three distinct biomes for the player to explore. The player gathers and extracts resources and uses them to craft progressively more complicated items and even structures.

The game was built using Unity, as such game code was developed in c#. Some of the assets were obtained on the Unity marketplace, those created from scratch were modeled and textured in blender.

Unfortunately I have lost the source files for this project over the years. I was a lot less careful about backing things up in the past. However, I did at least keep a windows build of the game. It can be obtained in the link below if you are interested.

Eclipse

This project was something that could be loosely described as a "game", lets just say it was a "good learning opportunity". My partner and I put this together as our submission for the 2016 FBLA real-time simulation and game design Maryland state competition. It had a lot of problems, unfortunately not among the least of which was the fact that didn't really have much of an objective, the gameplay was terrible, and all in all it was more of a technical demo than it was a game. We definitely suffered from a lack of perspective on this project concerning the time we had and what could be achieved in that time. Somehow though, by the grace of one judge who played the game and our incredible in-person presentation, we managed to place and go to nationals, where we decided to completely scrap this project and created Wilderness Survival which was an astronomically better game.

The "game" was built using Unity, as such game code was developed in c#.

I think I was probably so ashamed of this thing after the states competition that I either deleted the project, or "neglected" to back it up. It doesn't exist anymore. However, digging through some old things I found a flashdrive that contained files from our in-person presentation we gave at the state competition. I've decided to include this item in the catalog as its an important part of my developer story.

You may note the weird looking spaceship image in the carousel. Unfortunately back then I didn't know of a good way to render wireframes of models in blender. So what I came up with was... well that.
